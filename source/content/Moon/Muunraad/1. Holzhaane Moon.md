> [!quote] **Jesse Tobin**
> The Woodcock Moon begins with the first new moon after the end of winter. In the natural world at this time we can find the woodcock involved in its complex mating dance at sunsets in open areas near water. The time of hunger is over for those who know the ways of the woodcock. The woodcock lives in marginal lands and finds food where others can’t. The woodcock teaches us to adapt to our spring environment and find the new greens and spring foods to cleanse our bodies after the long winter. The woodcock teaches us to take advantage of opportunities given at this time, and to act with speed and timeliness. This is a time of plowing and preparation of our fields for the abundance that is to come.
