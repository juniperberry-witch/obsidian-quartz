Midsummer is the longest day of the year. Although we often try to frame this day as representing the victory of light over darkness, the reality is that both light and darkness play important roles in our lives and in the world and universe around us. Being attuned to the cycles of life and the rhythms that guide the streams of life is an important facet of Urglaawe philosophy. In the dead of winter, we are typically isolated and introspective. Deitsch tradition tells us that at those times, we are to assess ourselves, our deeds, our desires, and to find ways to improve our lives.

We are now on the opposite end of the year. We have planted the seeds for things we have set out to do, whether it be growth, change, or even increased stability. This is the time to enjoy life… to be social… to reach out to others and strengthen the bonds of community, to revel in the miracles and wonders of life and everything that it has to offer. This is a time to look at the things in life that weigh us down and to recognize that we must not allow burdens to cause the joys of life to pass us by. Look for signs that guide us towards an understanding of the reasons that things, (bad and good) happen and what role the events, people, change, emotions, etc., will play in the next phase of our lives. If nothing else, for one day, or for a few hours, let go of the burdens… Celebrate life with the people who have gathered here in common purpose.
